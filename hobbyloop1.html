Overview

HobbyLoop is a mobile application concept designed to bridge the gap between people holding highly specific or niche interests and active local communities. The goal of the project was to design an intuitive, frictionless system where users can easily organize, discover, and build safe communities around their favorite hobbies.

The Challenge

Finding local individuals who share unique passions can be surprisingly challenging. Modern social platforms are built around massive global networks or algorithms that optimize for casual media scrolling rather than facilitating real-life community coordination. Users often struggle to find safe, localized hubs to plan niche events or share hyper-specific knowledge.

User Research & Synthesis

To dive deep into the user experience, I conducted targeted user research to identify pain points and user patterns regarding community engagement:

  • Fragmented Identity: Users find it annoying to cross-reference multiple communication platforms (such as Discord, Reddit, and text threads) just to stay organized.
  • Safety Hurdles: Users are highly cautious about meeting new people offline without a formal verification layer or community validation score.
  • Discovery Friction: Niche group discovery is often reliant on pure luck or inside word-of-mouth networks.

Ideation & Wireframing

Shifting from insights to architecture, I drafted clean user paths focused on minimizing the number of clicks required to join a local group or spin up a new hobby event circle. The structural priority was creating clear navigation tabs separating discover feeds, individual group channels, and scheduled calendars.
